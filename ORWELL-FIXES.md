## Orwell's Rules Applied

### Rule 1: No dead metaphors
- ❌ "match your frequency"
- ❌ "build your path"
- ❌ "inverts this architecture"
- ✅ Use literal language

### Rule 2: Short words
- ❌ "autonomous programming" → ✅ "workshops, talks, dinners"
- ❌ "discoverable" → ✅ "easy to find"
- ❌ "aggregated" → ✅ "pulled from" or "collected from"

### Rule 3: Cut unnecessary words
- ❌ "elevating the overall fidelity of" → ✅ "improving"
- ❌ "acts as the physical core" → ✅ "is the hub"
- ❌ "through the central protocol" → ✅ DELETE (adds nothing)

### Rule 4: Active voice
- ❌ "events are hosted" → ✅ "hosts run events"
- ❌ "can be discovered" → ✅ "people find"
- ❌ "are aggregated" → ✅ "we pull"

### Rule 5: No jargon
- ❌ "protocol", "node", "ecosystem", "bandwidth", "fidelity"
- ✅ Use when it serves the brand (protocol, node = part of positioning)
- ✅ Cut when it's just showing off (ecosystem, bandwidth, fidelity)

### Rule 6: Break rules when necessary
- Keep "protocol" and "node" → they're part of the brand identity
- Keep "peer-to-peer" → signals the distributed model
- Cut everything else

## Rewritten Copy

### HOME - Thesis Section

**Original:**
> Traditional tech conferences put everyone in a dark room to listen to a single broadcast. Emergence inverts this architecture.
>
> For three days, offices, cafes, warehouses, and studios across London become active nodes in the network. Each node hosts autonomous programming—workshops, debates, hackathons, dinners—governed by the hosts but discoverable through the central protocol.
>
> This is not a top-down event. It's a peer-to-peer protocol for London's tech ecosystem to map itself, share bandwidth, and build unexpected connections. You don't just attend Emergence; you plug into it.

**Orwell-ized:**
> Traditional tech conferences put everyone in a dark room. Emergence does the opposite.
>
> For three days, offices, cafes, and warehouses across London host events: workshops, talks, hackathons, dinners. Each host runs their own programming. We make them all easy to find.
>
> This isn't one event. It's dozens of events, all happening at once. You don't attend Emergence. You navigate it.

### HOME - Host Card

**Original:**
> Have an office, a warehouse, or a living room? Turn your space into an active node. You control the format, the capacity, and the agenda. We provide the protocol and route the network to your door.

**Orwell-ized:**
> Have a space? Host an event. You control the format, capacity, and agenda. We list your event. People find it.

### HOME - Attend Card

**Original:**
> No single ticket. Build your own journey through the city. Sync your calendar with nodes that match your frequency. Navigate between micro-summits, underground demos, and rooftop debates.

**Orwell-ized:**
> No single ticket. Choose your events across London. Add them to your calendar. Navigate between talks, demos, and dinners.

### HOME - Sponsor Card

**Original:**
> Sponsors don't get logos on lanyards. They fund the ecosystem: supporting valuable programming, subsidizing costs for indie developers, and elevating the overall fidelity of the network.

**Orwell-ized:**
> Sponsors don't get logos on lanyards. They fund the platform: paying for events, covering costs for indie devs, making better programming possible.

### HOME - Kachette Section

**Original:**
> While the network is distributed, every system needs a central router. Located in the historic railway arches of Shoreditch, Kachette acts as the physical core. Come here to sync, collect credentials, and access the master schedule.

**Orwell-ized:**
> While events happen everywhere, every system needs a hub. Kachette is that hub. Located in Shoreditch's railway arches. Get your pass here. See the full schedule.

## Summary

**Words to cut entirely:**
- "ecosystem" (use "platform" or "network")
- "bandwidth" (meaningless metaphor)
- "fidelity" (pompous)
- "autonomous" (jargon for jargon's sake)
- "discoverable through the central protocol" (just say "easy to find")

**Words to keep (brand identity):**
- "protocol" (core to positioning)
- "node" (core to positioning)
- "distributed" (describes the model)
- "peer-to-peer" (when it clarifies structure)

**Passive → Active changes:**
- "events are aggregated" → "we pull events"
- "can be discovered" → "people find"
- "is hosted" → "hosts run"

**Abstract → Concrete:**
- "autonomous programming" → "workshops, talks, dinners"
- "match your frequency" → "events you care about"
- "elevating fidelity" → "making better events"
